Mobo Code Website

Design and development of website from concept to completion for Web Design 1 course.

View a live version of the site

View the project files on Git Hub

During the great depression of the 1930s there were countless people who lost their jobs and their homes and took to traveling. These people, known as hobos, developed a system of symbols to communicate to each other warnings and tips for surviving on the streets. Hobos were shunned as outcasts from society and relied on the help they could get from other people in a similar situation.

Nowadays, there is still a multitude of homeless and/or nomadic people living in our cities, but I believe people that are more fortunate are more understanding of the plight and willing to help those that are less fortunate. Especially in recent years with so many people in the U.S. struggling with economic hardship.

What if there was a modern system of symbols that people living on the streets could instantly recognize for helpful resources?

Purpose

This site represents a system of symbols designed to be used by businesses, churches, libraries, etc. that are sympathetic to the hardships that homeless people face to easily communicate what resources they can offer. These symbols should be displayed in a window, door, or other easily seen location so that passers-by who understood these symbols would know what resources they are willing to offer.

Examples of what these places could offer include available public restrooms, free food that would otherwise be thrown out at the end of the day and what time of the day it is available, free computer usage, etc.

The purpose of this site is to raise awareness of this system of symbols, but also be a place where businesses who are willing to help can obtain the symbols and list their business in a directory or map that people who are searching for resources can find.

Aesthetic

The overall aesthetic of using the ripped cardboard background image and the felt-tip marker style font replicates the cardboard signs that many homeless people use as a means of communicating their message. Since the purpose of this site is to create another system of communication, it is only appropriate to tie into the concept.

The colors used are modern, urban, and earthy.
